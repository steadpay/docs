SteadyPay is a billing enforcement layer that sits between your Stripe account and your application. When a subscriber's payment fails, SteadyPay catches them at the highest-intent moment — their next login — and presents a non-bypassable card-update overlay. No dunning emails. No grace-period guessing. Just recovered revenue.

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Stripe fires a webhook">
    When a payment fails, Stripe sends an `invoice.payment_failed` event to SteadyPay's webhook endpoint.
  </Step>
  <Step title="SteadyPay updates the subscriber's status">
    SteadyPay classifies the failure as a soft decline (retriable) or hard decline (card rejected outright) and marks the subscriber as **warning** or **lockout** accordingly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Your app checks status on every session">
    The enforcement snippet or mobile SDK calls the SteadyPay status API on each authenticated page load using the subscriber's Stripe customer ID.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Subscriber updates their card">
    A **warning** subscriber sees a dismissable banner; a **lockout** subscriber sees a full-screen Stripe Payment Element overlay they cannot bypass. Either way, updating the card resolves the gate and resumes the session.
  </Step>
</Steps>
