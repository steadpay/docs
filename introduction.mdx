SteadyPay is a billing enforcement layer for Stripe-powered SaaS products. Instead of relying on dunning emails that subscribers ignore, SteadyPay catches failed payments at the highest-intent moment — when the subscriber is already inside your app — and presents a gate they must resolve before continuing.

## What SteadyPay does

When a subscriber's card fails, SteadyPay intercepts their next login with a non-bypassable overlay that requires them to update their card before they can use your product. The overlay loads a Stripe-hosted Payment Element directly, so card data never touches your servers. Once the subscriber updates their card, the gate dismisses and the session resumes immediately.

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Stripe fires invoice.payment_failed">
    Stripe sends the `invoice.payment_failed` webhook to SteadyPay's endpoint. SteadyPay inspects the decline code and classifies the failure as either a **soft decline** (retriable — insufficient funds, temporary network issue) or a **hard decline** (card rejected outright — blocked card, fraud flag).
  </Step>
  <Step title="SteadyPay updates the subscriber's status">
    Based on the classification, SteadyPay moves the subscriber to **warning** (soft decline, retry scheduled) or **lockout** (hard decline or retries exhausted). The status is immediately available via the status API.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Your app checks status on every session">
    On each authenticated page load or app launch, the enforcement snippet or mobile SDK sends the subscriber's `stripe_customer_id` to the SteadyPay status API. This call is lightweight and cached at the edge for performance.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Subscriber sees the appropriate enforcement surface">
    A **warning** subscriber sees a dismissable banner prompting them to update their card. A **lockout** subscriber sees a full-screen Stripe Payment Element overlay — there is no way to dismiss it without completing a successful card update.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Subscriber statuses

Every subscriber tracked by SteadyPay carries exactly one of three statuses at any given time:

| Status | Meaning | What the subscriber sees |
|--------|---------|--------------------------|
| **Active** | Payment is current; no enforcement in effect | Nothing — the app loads normally |
| **Warning** | Soft decline received; retry is scheduled | A dismissable banner prompting a card update |
| **Lockout** | Hard decline received, or all retries exhausted | A full-screen overlay that cannot be dismissed |

<Note>
  A subscriber transitions from **warning** to **lockout** automatically if the final retry on Day 7 fails. You do not need to manage this transition manually.
</Note>

## Prerequisites

Before you integrate SteadyPay, make sure you have the following:

- A **Stripe account in live mode** — SteadyPay reads real invoice and decline data from Stripe's webhook stream.
- A **SteadyPay account** — [sign up at steadpayhq.com/signup](https://steadpayhq.com/signup) and connect Stripe via OAuth during onboarding.
- Your **subscriber's `stripe_customer_id`** available at login time — SteadyPay uses this identifier to look up and enforce the subscriber's billing status.
